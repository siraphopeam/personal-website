I spent nine years in agencies. Now I'm in-house, making real numbers move.

I started as a junior strategic planner and worked my way up to director — running brand and growth work for clients across categories. It was a great education, but the gap between strategy decks and shipped results kept getting wider.

So I traded the agency life for peace of mind and a chance to build the whole loop: research, strategy, campaigns, data, iteration. Today I lead marketing for an electronic-appliance brand — and I treat it like a long, careful experiment.
